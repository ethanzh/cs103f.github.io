Blog Post 4

Computer science has, for the most part, always been a male-dominated field. Of course this is changing now, but decades ago (when the current GitHub and Uber employees were going through school) there were very few females in the mix, thus becoming a sort of "boys club". As one would expect, this culture permeated through university, and into the companies that these students went on to join. The sheer length of time that CS was male-dominated has made it hard for females to feel welcomed. This leads to issues such as men not trusting code that was written by a woman.

The common thread in both the Uber and Github stories is that the systems put in place to prevent harassment and discrimination failed completely. The HR department at Github took the side of the high ranking executives over Hovrath despite clear evidence of harassment while Uber's HR department straight up admitted to dismissing Fowler's complaints because the offending persons were "high performers." The HR departments acted this way because they exist to serve the company and by extension it's profit, so they will always act in a way that most serves the company's interests even if that comes at the expense of its lower to middle level employees.

In order for the culture of marginalizing women within the tech industry to be dismantled, those who perpetrate these biases must recognize their actions. Through the lens of virtue based ethics, those working in the tech industry will not discriminate against marginalized demographics but instead focus on the positive aspects of the person's work abilities and characteristics. Women must also continue to support other women, advocate for one another, and help one another surpass any obstacles that may come their way. Through duty-based ethics, people should also speak up when they witness gender discrimination occurring or are involved in any such scenario. This will inspire others to also speak up and bring about a change in the often unwelcoming atmosphere for women in the tech industry.
